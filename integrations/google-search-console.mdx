## Overview

Google Search Console shows how your site performs in Google search results. Connect it to Hazel to track your organic search visibility — which keywords you rank for, which pages get the most clicks, and where you have room to grow.

## What Hazel pulls

Once you connect Google Search Console, Hazel has access to:

- **Keyword performance** — search terms that trigger your site, with impressions, clicks, CTR, and average position
- **Page performance** — which pages on your site get the most search traffic
- **Site-level metrics** — overall search visibility and click trends
- **Sitemap status** — indexing health of your site

## How to connect

Find Google Search Console in the [Hazel marketplace](https://app.fromhazel.ai/integrations/marketplace?search=google+search+console) and click the **+** button. You'll sign in through Google in a popup window — use the account that has access to your Search Console property.

**What you'll need:** A Google account with access to your Search Console property.

## Example questions you can ask

Once your Google Search Console data is connected, try questions like these:

- "What are our top keywords by clicks this month?"
- "Which pages have high impressions but low CTR? (Opportunities to improve title tags)"
- "How has our organic search traffic trended over the last 90 days?"
- "What new keywords are we starting to rank for?"
- "Show me our average position for branded vs non-branded keywords"
- "Which pages have the most search impressions?"

## Good to know

<Note>
  Google Search Console data has a **2-3 day reporting delay** — this is a Google limitation, not a Hazel one. The most recent data available is typically from 2-3 days ago.
</Note>

- Some search terms are redacted by Google and show as "(not provided)" — this is standard Google behavior to protect user privacy.
- Pair with Google Analytics to connect organic search performance to on-site behavior and conversions.
- Your data refreshes automatically.

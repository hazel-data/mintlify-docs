## Overview

If you sell on Amazon, this integration gives you the full picture — orders, inventory, FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon), and advertising data. With 121+ data tables, Amazon is one of Hazel's most data-rich integrations.

## What Hazel pulls

Once you connect Amazon, Hazel has access to:

- **Orders** — every Amazon order with line items, fulfillment status, and fees
- **Inventory levels** — real-time stock counts across your catalog
- **FBA data** — Fulfillment by Amazon metrics including storage fees, removal orders, and inbound shipments
- **Advertising campaigns** — Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Sponsored Display performance data
- **121+ data tables** — covering orders, ads, inventory, fees, and more

## How to connect

Find Amazon in the [Hazel marketplace](https://app.fromhazel.ai/integrations/marketplace?search=amazon) and click the **+** button. You'll sign in through Amazon Seller Central to authorize the connection.

**What you'll need:** An Amazon Seller Central account with access to the data you want to analyze.

## Example questions you can ask

Once your Amazon data is connected, try questions like these:

- "What's our Amazon revenue this month vs Shopify?"
- "Show me inventory levels for our top 10 ASINs"
- "Which Sponsored Products campaigns have the best ROAS?"
- "Are any products at risk of going out of stock?"
- "What are our FBA storage fees this quarter?"
- "Compare our Amazon advertising spend vs return"

## Good to know

<Note>
  Amazon is one of Hazel's **most data-rich integrations** with 121+ tables. The initial backfill may take longer than other integrations — give it a few days to complete.
</Note>

- Pair with Shopify to compare performance across your direct and Amazon channels side by side.
- For Amazon advertising data (Sponsored Products, Brands, and Display), see the dedicated [Amazon Ads](/integrations/amazon-ads) integration page.
- **Amazon customers are separate from Shopify customers** — Hazel can't deduplicate customers across Amazon and Shopify because Amazon doesn't share customer email addresses. You can compare the channels, but can't track the same person across both.
- Your data refreshes automatically.

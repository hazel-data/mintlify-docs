## OAuth vs API key

There are two ways integrations authenticate:

- **OAuth** — You click "Connect" and sign in through the platform's own login screen (like signing in to Meta or Google). This is the most common method and the easiest. You don't need to copy or paste anything.

- **API key** — Some platforms use API keys instead. You'll copy a key from that platform's settings and paste it into Hazel. We'll walk you through exactly where to find it.

Both methods are secure. Hazel only requests the permissions it needs to read your data.

<Tip>
  Not sure which auth type your integration uses? Just click **Connect** — Hazel will guide you through the right flow automatically.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My data isn't syncing">
    First, check that your integration shows as **Connected** in **Integrations** in the side nav. If it says "Error" or "Disconnected," try disconnecting and reconnecting. Some common causes: your password changed, your API key was rotated, or permissions were revoked on the platform's side.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I don't see my data yet">
    Initial backfills can take a few hours to a few days, especially for platforms with a lot of historical data (like Shopify or Amazon). If it's been more than a few days and you still don't see data, reach out via your shared Slack channel.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I have multiple Shopify stores">
    Hazel supports multiple Shopify stores. Connect each store separately from **Integrations** in the side nav. When you ask questions, Hazel will know which store the data comes from, and you can ask about them individually or together.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I disconnect a source?">
    Yes. Go to **Integrations** in the side nav, find the connected source, and click **Disconnect**. Your historical data will be removed. You can always reconnect later and Hazel will backfill again.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Overview

Stripe powers payments for many DTC brands. Connect it to Hazel to analyze charges, subscriptions, refunds, and revenue — especially useful if you run a subscription program or sell through channels beyond Shopify.

## What Hazel pulls

Once you connect Stripe, Hazel has access to:

- **Charges and payments** — every successful and failed charge with amounts and metadata
- **Refunds** — refund amounts, reasons, and timing
- **Subscriptions** — active, canceled, and paused subscriptions with plan details
- **Invoices** — invoice history and payment status
- **Customers** — customer profiles linked to their payment history
- **Disputes** — chargebacks and dispute outcomes

## How to connect

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings > Integrations">
    Open **Settings** in Hazel and click **Integrations**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find Stripe and click Connect">
    Search for Stripe or find it in the Finance category.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Authorize via Stripe">
    You'll be redirected to Stripe to authorize the connection. Make sure you connect the correct Stripe account if you have multiple.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example questions you can ask

Once your Stripe data is connected, try questions like these:

- "What's our MRR from subscriptions?"
- "Show me refund trends over the last 90 days"
- "How many active subscriptions do we have?"
- "What's our dispute rate this quarter?"
- "What's the average subscription lifetime?"
- "Compare subscription revenue vs one-time purchases"

## Good to know

- **MRR** stands for Monthly Recurring Revenue — the predictable revenue you earn each month from active subscriptions.
- Pair with Shopify to see your complete revenue picture across subscription and one-time purchases.
- Your data refreshes every 4 hours automatically.

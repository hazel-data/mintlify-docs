## Overview

Shopify is the foundation for most Hazel customers. It's usually the first integration people connect — and for good reason. Your Shopify data powers revenue analysis, customer insights, product performance, and much more.

## What Hazel pulls

Once you connect Shopify, Hazel has access to:

- **Orders** — every order, including line items, discounts applied, and order status
- **Customers** — customer profiles, order history, and lifetime value
- **Products & variants** — your full product catalog with pricing, inventory, and variant details
- **Fulfillments** — shipping and delivery status for every order
- **Refunds & returns** — refund amounts, reasons, and which products get returned most
- **Discounts** — discount codes and automatic discounts, including usage and revenue impact
- **Collections** — how your products are organized
- **Metafields** — any custom data you've added to orders, products, or customers

## Example questions you can ask

Once your Shopify data is connected, try questions like these:

- "What was our revenue last month?"
- "Show me our top 10 products by units sold"
- "What's our average order value trend over the last 90 days?"
- "Which products have the highest return rate?"
- "Compare new vs returning customer revenue this quarter"
- "What's our repeat purchase rate?"
- "How many orders used a discount code last month, and what was the average discount?"
- "Show me revenue by day for the last 30 days"

## Good to know

<Note>
  Hazel syncs your **full order history** — not just recent data. That means you can ask about trends over months or even years.
</Note>

- Your data refreshes every 4 hours automatically.
- Hazel supports **multiple Shopify stores**. Connect each one separately and ask about them individually or together.
- Shopify data pairs beautifully with your ad platforms. Connect Meta Ads or Google Ads to see true ROAS against actual Shopify revenue.

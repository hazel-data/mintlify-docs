---
title: "Quick Ask vs Deep Thinking"
description: "Choose the right mode for your question — fast tactical answers or deep strategic research."
---

Hazel has two modes for answering questions. Picking the right one means you get the best answer in the right amount of time.

## Quick Ask

Quick Ask gives you answers in seconds. It's the default mode and the one you'll use most often.

Quick Ask is best for tactical, straightforward questions where you need a fast answer:

- "What was revenue yesterday?"
- "Show me our top SKU this month"
- "How many orders did we get last week?"
- "What's our current email subscriber count?"

Think of Quick Ask as asking a coworker who has all your dashboards open. They glance at the screen and give you the number.

## Deep Thinking

Deep Thinking takes a few minutes but delivers multi-step research that would normally take you hours.

When you ask a Deep Thinking question, Hazel builds a research plan and shows it to you before executing. You can review the plan, adjust it, and then let Hazel run the full analysis across your data.

Deep Thinking is best for strategic, complex questions that require pulling from multiple sources:

- "Analyze our customer retention by acquisition channel over the last 6 months"
- "What's driving the increase in our CAC (cost to acquire a new customer)?"
- "Compare our product margins across categories and recommend where to focus"
- "Build a cohort analysis of customers acquired during our last sale"

Think of Deep Thinking as handing a research project to a sharp analyst. They come back with a thorough report, not just a number.

## When to use which

| Question type | Mode | Time | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single metric lookup | Quick Ask | Seconds | "What was revenue last week?" |
| Simple comparison | Quick Ask | Seconds | "Compare this week to last week" |
| Trend over time | Quick Ask | Seconds | "Show me daily orders for the last 30 days" |
| Multi-step analysis | Deep Thinking | A few minutes | "What's driving our rising CAC?" |
| Cross-source research | Deep Thinking | A few minutes | "Analyze retention by acquisition channel" |

<Tip>
  Not sure which to use? Start with Quick Ask. If the answer needs more depth, Hazel may suggest switching to Deep Thinking — or you can ask for it: "Can you do a deep analysis on this?"
</Tip>

## Trust, but verify

When Hazel gives you a number, you shouldn't have to take it on faith. That's why data points in Hazel's responses come with numbered citations you can check.

You'll see small numbered markers next to key figures. These aren't just footnotes — they show you exactly how Hazel arrived at each number.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hazel answers your question">
    You get a clear response with charts, tables, and key metrics — each with a numbered citation marker.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click any citation">
    Click the number to see a simple equation showing the calculation and the source(s) used to produce that figure.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify and share with confidence">
    Now you know exactly where every number came from. Share the analysis with your team or your board knowing it's traceable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why it matters

When you're presenting numbers to your leadership team, sharing performance with a brand partner, or making a budget decision, you need to know the data is solid.

Citations mean you can always answer the question: "Where did this number come from?" You'll never be stuck saying "I think it's from Shopify" — you'll know.

<Note>
  Citations are currently available in **Deep Thinking** mode, where they're especially important for complex multi-step research across multiple data sources. Every statistic in Deep Thinking reports is linked back to its source, so you can trace the full chain of analysis.
</Note>

## Trust, but verify

When Hazel gives you a number, you shouldn't have to take it on faith. That's why every data point in Hazel's responses comes with a numbered citation you can check.

You'll see small numbered markers next to key figures in every answer. These aren't just footnotes — they're direct links to the underlying query and data that produced each number.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hazel answers your question">
    You get a clear response with charts, tables, and key metrics — each with a numbered citation marker.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click any citation">
    Click the number to see exactly how Hazel arrived at that figure — the data source it pulled from, the query it ran, and the raw results.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify and share with confidence">
    Now you know exactly where every number came from. Share the analysis with your team or your board knowing it's traceable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why it matters

When you're presenting numbers to your leadership team, sharing performance with a brand partner, or making a budget decision, you need to know the data is solid.

Citations mean you can always answer the question: "Where did this number come from?" You'll never be stuck saying "I think it's from Shopify" — you'll know.

<Note>
  Citations are especially important for Deep Thinking responses, which involve complex multi-step research across multiple data sources. Every statistic in those longer reports is linked back to its source, so you can trace the full chain of analysis.
</Note>
